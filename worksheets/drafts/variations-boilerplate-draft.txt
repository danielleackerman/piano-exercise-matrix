================================================================
VARIATIONS
================================================================

    Contrary Motion
    Any scale or arpeggio exercise can be played in contrary
    motion — one hand ascending while the other descends
    simultaneously. This is harder than parallel motion and
    excellent brain training.


    Accent & Rhythmic Variations
    Apply to any scale or arpeggio exercise for fluidity and
    evenness:
    a  Accent every note — extra weight on each individual note.
    b  Accent every second note — grouped, rolling feel.
    c  Triplet groupings — organize the scale in groups of three.
    d  Groups of four — organize the scale in groups of four.
    e  Prolong one note per group — hold the 2nd or 3rd note
       slightly longer to build control.


    Staccato
    Any scale exercise can be played staccato — lift from each
    key rather than connecting the notes. Treat it as a separate
    pass, not a replacement for legato practice.
